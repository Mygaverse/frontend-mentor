UI/UX Design:

1. Overview:

This assignment presents the challenge of revamping a concert ticket booking system to enhance user experience and combat malicious activities that disrupt fair ticket access. My primary tasks are to:

  1. Design intuitive and user-friendly UI mockups
  2. Develop clear and comprehensive process flows 
  3. Craft a detailed specification document 

To tackle these tasks effectively, I'll adopt a user-centered approach, prioritizing the needs and preferences of genuine concert-goers. This involves:

  • Understanding user pain points: Identifying the challenges users face with the current system and addressing them in the redesign.
  • Balancing security and usability: Implementing robust anti-bot and anti-scalper measures without compromising the user experience.
  • Creating a rewarding loyalty program: Encouraging user engagement and retention through a well-structured loyalty program with meaningful incentives.

2. Problem Statement:

The current concert ticket booking system faces two critical challenges:

  1. Malicious Activities: Bots and scalpers exploit the system to purchase large quantities of tickets, preventing real fans from accessing events and driving up prices on the secondary market. This leads to frustration, decreased customer satisfaction, and potential revenue loss for the platform.
  2. Limited User Engagement: The platform lacks mechanisms to foster user loyalty and encourage repeat purchases. This results in missed opportunities to build a strong community and maximize customer lifetime value.

3. Solution Overview:

  1. Anti-Bot/Anti-Scalper Measures:
  1. Implement robust CAPTCHA challenges at critical points in the booking process (e.g., ticket selection, checkout) to deter automated ticket purchases.
  2. Integrate phone verification to ensure that purchases are made by genuine individuals, tying transactions to unique phone numbers.
  3. Employ rate limiting to restrict excessive activity and prevent brute-force attacks.
  4. Develop a system to detect and flag suspicious activity patterns in real-time, temporarily blocking users exhibiting bot-like or scalper behavior.
  1. Loyalty Program:
  1. Introduce a tiered loyalty program that rewards users for various actions, including ticket purchases, referrals, social media engagement, and completing profile tasks (e.g., KYC verification).
  2. Offer a variety of redemption options, including discounts on ticket purchases, early access to ticket sales, exclusive merchandise or events, and partner coupons.
  3. Prominently display the user's loyalty status and point balance near their avatar or profile icon, providing easy access to program details and progress tracking.

5. Ideation and Concept Development:

  1. Anti-Bot/Scalper Measures

  • Key Concepts:
  • CAPTCHA:
  • Implement Google reCAPTCHA with its various challenge types (image recognition, checkbox, etc.).  
  • Explore alternative CAPTCHA options like simple math problems or time-based questions ("What is the current month?").  
  • Consider a two-tier CAPTCHA system: an initial simple challenge for everyone, followed by a more complex one if suspicious activity is detected.  
  • Audio CAPTCHA: For accessibility, offer an audio version where users type the numbers or words they hear.
  • Phone and Email Verification:
  • Require phone number verification via SMS code at the time of purchase.  
  • Implement email verification for account creation, but be aware of potential limitations (bots can create multiple accounts).  
  • Consider allowing users to link their social media accounts for faster verification, but weigh the privacy implications.  
  • Rate Limiting and Activity Monitoring:
  • Set strict rate limits on actions like browsing events, adding to cart, and checkout attempts.  
  • Monitor user activity patterns (e.g., mouse movements, typing speed) to detect bot-like behavior.  
  • Implement a "waiting room" system for high-demand events to manage traffic and prevent overload.  
  • Ideation:

The best approach might be a combination of methods. Perhaps a primary verification step (like email or a simple CAPTCHA) followed by a secondary step for suspicious activity (like phone verification) could be more effective.

Things need to be considered for further refine this strategy:

  • Timing of Verification: When would be the most effective point in the user journey to implement each verification step? Perhaps the picture/math CAPTCHA at the start of the booking process, and the phone verification just before finalizing the purchase?
  • Error Handling: What happens if a user makes a mistake in the picture/math CAPTCHA? How many attempts do they get? How is this communicated clearly and in a user-friendly way?
  • Accessibility: While picture recognition is generally accessible, consider offering alternatives like audio CAPTCHA for visually impaired users.

Integrating the CAPTCHA and phone verification steps into specific points in the user journey: Placing the picture/math/audio CAPTCHA right after login effectively filters out bots early on, preventing them from even accessing the booking process. And triggering the phone verification just before payment adds a strong layer of security against scalpers at a critical point.

Limiting verification attempts to 5 strikes would be a good balance between security and user experience. However, consider adding clear messaging to guide users through the process:

  1. Visual Cues: A number countdown or progress bar could show users how many attempts they have left.
  2. Helpful Error Messages: Instead of just saying "Incorrect code," provide specific feedback like, "3 attempts remaining. Please listen carefully and try again."
  3. Alternative Options: If users exhaust their attempts, offer a way to contact customer support for assistance. This ensures legitimate users aren't locked out due to simple errors or technical glitches.

  • Ideation:

Loyalty program is integrated into the user interface and overall flow:

  • Visibility: Where and how will users see their loyalty points and status?
  • Earning Points: What actions will earn users points (e.g., purchases, referrals, social media engagement)?
  • Redeeming Points: How will users redeem points for discounts or early access? Will there be different tiers or rewards?

Integrating the loyalty points and status directly into the user's avatar or profile icon. This ensures high visibility and consistent access for users, regardless of which page they're on. It's a subtle yet effective way to keep users engaged and informed about their progress.

By offering a variety of ways to earn points – purchases, referrals, social media engagement, and even completing KYC –  encourage active participation and reward users for different types of interactions. This creates a more dynamic and rewarding experience.

Assigning fixed points for different actions provides a transparent and predictable system for users to understand how they earn rewards. Incorporating a percentage-based point accrual for purchases further encourages spending and adds value for frequent ticket buyers.

The tiered approach to redeeming points adds an element of gamification and aspiration, motivating users to strive for higher levels and unlock better rewards. Offering exclusive benefits like special events for higher-level users further enhances the appeal of the loyalty program.

Other practical aspects need to be consider:

  • Point Calculation: How will you calculate points for different actions? Will it be a fixed value or a percentage of the purchase amount?
  • Tier Thresholds: What are the point thresholds for each level? How will you balance exclusivity with attainability?
  • Reward Variety: What other rewards could you offer besides discounts and early access? Think about merchandise, meet-and-greets, or exclusive content.
  • Progress Visualization: Incorporate a progress bar or visual indicator on the user's profile page to clearly show their current level, point balance, and how far they are from reaching the next tier.
  • Personalized Recommendations: Use data on user preferences and past purchases to suggest relevant events, merchandise, or rewards they might be interested in.
  • Limited-Time Challenges: Introduce occasional challenges or contests with bonus points or exclusive rewards to create a sense of urgency and excitement.

Specification Document:

1, Feature Overviews:

  • Anti-Bot/Anti-Scalper Mechanisms:

  • CAPTCHA Verification: To prevent automated ticket purchases (Anti-Bots), a CAPTCHA challenge will be implemented on the ticket selection page. This will ensure that only human users can proceed to add tickets to their cart and attempt to purchase them.
  • Phone Verification: To deter scalpers and ensure that tickets are purchased by genuine individuals (Anti-Scalper), phone verification will be integrated into the order summary page. This measure adds a layer of security by tying the purchase to a unique phone number, making it more difficult for scalpers to use automated tools or multiple accounts to acquire large quantities of tickets.  
  • Detecting and Flagging Suspicious Activity Patterns: In addition to CAPTCHA and phone verification, the system will implement real-time monitoring and analysis of user activity patterns to detect and flag suspicious behavior that may indicate bot or scalper activity. This feature aims to proactively identify potentially harmful actions and the account will be temporarily flagged for review before they impact the system or other users.
  • Rate Limiting: To prevent brute-force attacks and suspicious activity patterns that may indicate bot or scalper activity, rate limiting will be implemented on various user actions throughout the concert ticket booking system. Rate limiting restricts the number of times a specific action can be performed within a given timeframe, effectively thwarting attempts to overwhelm the system or bypass security measures.

  • Loyalty Program:

  • Earning Points: The loyalty program aims to reward users for their engagement and incentivize actions that benefit both the users and the platform. Users can earn points through various activities, including ticket purchases, referrals, social media engagement, and completing profile tasks such as KYC. This approach not only encourages active participation but also reinforces the platform's security and ecosystem.  
  • Redeeming Points: Loyalty points accumulated by users can be redeemed for various rewards. The primary redemption method is through discounts on ticket purchases during the checkout process. Additionally, points can be redeemed for other valuable rewards such as early access to ticket sales, exclusive merchandise or events, and partner coupons.  
  • Status Visibility and Tiered System: To keep users engaged and informed about their loyalty program progress, their status and point balance will be prominently displayed near their avatar or profile icon. Additionally, a detailed loyalty points history, including a record of awarded tasks and progress, will be available on the user profile page. A tiered system with increasing benefits based on user level (e.g., Bronze, Silver, Gold) will further incentivize engagement and reward loyal users.

  • Functional Requirements
    These define what the system should do.

  1. Anti-Bot/Anti-Scalper Measures:

  2. The system shall provide clear instructions and feedback during the CAPTCHA process.
  3. The system shall monitor user activity patterns (e.g., purchase frequency, clickstream data, account activity).
  4. The system shall flag suspicious behavior based on predefined rules or machine learning models.
  5. The system shall temporarily restrict checkout access for flagged accounts.
  6. The system shall initiate a manual review process for flagged accounts.
  7. The system shall allow a limited number of retries before temporarily blocking the user.
  8. The system shall limit the number of times a user can perform specific actions (browsing, adding to cart, checkout) within a given timeframe.
  9. The system shall provide clear error messages to users when they exceed rate limits.
  1. Loyalty Program:
  1. The system shall award points for ticket purchases based on a defined scheme (e.g., fixed points per ticket, percentage of purchase amount).
  2. The system shall award bonus points for successful referrals who make a purchase.
  3. The system shall award points for social media engagement (e.g., sharing event details, following the platform's page).
  4. The system shall award points for completing profile tasks (e.g., KYC verification, payment method verification).
  5. The system shall allow users to redeem points for discounts on ticket purchases during checkout.
  6. The system shall clearly display the discount applied to the order total.
  7. The system shall deduct redeemed points from the user's balance after a successful purchase.
  8. The system shall allow points to be redeemed for other rewards (early access, merchandise, events, partner coupons).
  9. The system shall prominently display the user's loyalty status (e.g., tier level) and point balance.
  10. This information shall be visible near the user's avatar or profile icon across various pages.
  11. The user profile page shall include a dedicated section for loyalty program details.
  12. This section shall display a history of earned points, including a breakdown of activities and corresponding points.
  13. The section shall show the user's progress towards higher tiers in the loyalty program.

  • Anti-bot/Anti-scalper mechanisms

  • CAPTCHA verification

User Actions

  1. The user browses events and selects an event to view details.
  2. The user chooses the desired number of tickets and seating area (if applicable).
  3. The user clicks the "Add to Cart" button.

System Responses

  1. The system generates and displays a CAPTCHA challenge. The CAPTCHA type may vary (image recognition, simple math problem, audio) based on user preference or accessibility needs.
  2. The user is prompted to complete the CAPTCHA challenge.

Conditions

Scenario 1: Successful CAPTCHA Completion

User Action:

The user successfully completes the CAPTCHA challenge.

System Response:

The system validates the CAPTCHA input.

Condition:

If the CAPTCHA input is correct, the user is redirected to the order summary page.

Scenario 2: Incorrect CAPTCHA Completion

User Action:

The user submits an incorrect CAPTCHA response.

System Response:

The system validates the CAPTCHA input.

Condition:

If the CAPTCHA input is incorrect, the user is shown an error message and prompted to try again.

Scenario 3: Multiple CAPTCHA Failures

User Action:

The user submits incorrect CAPTCHA responses multiple times (e.g., 5 times).

System Response

The system tracks the number of failed attempts.

Condition:

If the number of failed attempts exceeds a predefined limit (e.g., 5), the user is temporarily blocked from the booking process. The user may be presented with options to contact customer support or try again later.

User Actions

Conditions

Scenario 1: Successful Code Entry

User Action:

The user enters the correct verification code.

System Response:

The system validates the entered code against the code sent via SMS.

Condition:

If the entered code matches the sent code, the user is redirected to the payment page to complete the purchase.

Scenario 2: Incorrect Code Entry

User Action:

The user enters an incorrect verification code.

System Response:

The system validates the entered code.

Condition:

If the entered code does not match the sent code, the user is shown an error message and prompted to try again.

Scenario 3: Multiple Code Entry Failures

User Action:

The user enters incorrect verification codes multiple times (e.g., 5 times).

System Response

The system tracks the number of failed attempts.

Condition:

If the number of failed attempts exceeds a predefined limit (e.g., 5), the user is temporarily blocked from the booking process. The user may be presented with options to contact customer support or try again later.

User Actions

  1. The system monitors the frequency of user actions in real-time.  
  2. The system compares the user's activity against predefined rate limits (thresholds) for each action.

Conditions

User Action:

The user performs an action (browsing, adding to cart, checkout) at a rate that exceeds the defined limit.

System Response:

The system blocks the user's action and displays an error message.

Condition:

If the user's request rate exceeds the threshold, the system temporarily prevents them from repeating the action..

Scenario 2: Legitimate User Affected

User Action:

A legitimate user may inadvertently trigger rate limiting due to high activity or network issues.

System Response:

The system provides a clear error message explaining the rate limit and suggests alternative actions (e.g., waiting a short period before retrying).

Condition:

If the user's activity is genuinely legitimate, they can adjust their behavior or contact customer support for assistance.

User Actions

  1. The user adds tickets to their cart and proceeds to the checkout page.  
  2. The user views their loyalty point balance and available rewards.  
  3. The user selects a reward to redeem (e.g., discount on purchase, early access to future events).

System Responses

  1. The system displays the user's loyalty point balance and eligible rewards on the checkout page.  
  2. The system validates the user's eligibility for the selected reward.  
  3. The system applies the reward to the order (e.g., applies a discount, grants early access).

Conditions

Scenario 1: Successful Redemption

User Action:

The user selects a valid reward and has enough points to redeem it.

System Response:

The system applies the reward to the order and updates the order total.

Condition:

If the reward is a discount, the order total is reduced accordingly. If the reward is early access, the user is granted access to purchase tickets before the general public.

Scenario 2: Insufficient Points

User Action:

The user attempts to redeem a reward but does not have enough points.

System Response:

The system displays an error message indicating insufficient points and prevents the redemption.

Condition:

The user is prompted to choose a different reward or earn more points.

Scenario 3: Reward Not Applicable

User Action:

The user attempts to redeem a reward that is not applicable to their current order (e.g., early access to an event that is not on sale yet).

System Response

The system displays an error message indicating that the reward is not applicable..

Condition:

The user is prompted to choose a different reward or proceed with their order without redeeming points.

  • Detection and Restriction of Suspicious Patterns:

Positive Flows

Negative Flows

Scenario 1: Successful Detection and Restriction

  1. The system detects a user rapidly adding multiple tickets to their cart (potential bot activity).
  2. The user's account is flagged, and they are temporarily blocked from completing the checkout process.
  3. The suspicious activity is logged and sent for manual review.

Scenario 1: False Positive Handling

  1. A legitimate user triggers a suspicious activity alert due to a high purchase frequency (e.g., buying tickets for a large group).
  2. The user is temporarily blocked from checkout but provided with a clear explanation and instructions to contact customer support.
  3. The user successfully verifies their identity with customer support, and their access is restored.

Scenario 2: Accurate Identification of Scalper Patterns

  1. The system identifies a user making repeated purchases with different accounts and varying payment methods (potential scalping behavior).
  2. The user's accounts are flagged, and their access to checkout is restricted.
  3. The suspicious activity is escalated for manual review and potential further action (e.g., permanent ban).

Scenario 2: Adaptive Thresholds

  • The system initially flags a user for browsing a large number of events within a short timeframe.
  • Upon review, it is determined that this behavior is common during peak hours or for specific user types (e.g., event planners).
  • The system adjusts its detection thresholds to reduce false positives while still effectively identifying genuine threats.

Scenario 3: Evolving Detection

  • The system initially fails to detect a new form of suspicious activity (e.g., a novel bot technique).
  • The system is updated with new rules or machine learning models to recognize and flag the new pattern.
  • The updated system successfully detects and restricts suspicious activity.

Negative Flows

  1. Users attempt to make multiple checkout attempts with invalid payment information within a short timeframe.
  2. The system detects the repeated failed attempts and temporarily blocks the user from further checkout attempts.
  3. An error message is displayed, informing the user of the rate limit and suggesting they try again later or contact customer support.
